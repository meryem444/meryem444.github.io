Executive Summary

This is a working family's grand crossing — Austin's eleven-day Cairo engagement framed by a true family chapter in Türkiye. Choreographed in three acts, the journey balances the demands of an infant and a preschooler with the rare opportunity to gather three generations across two countries. The operative principle is simple: minimize transitions, lean into the family hosts in Türkiye, and let the Cairo heat dictate the early week's tempo. The remainder of this dossier exists so that the family does not need to think about logistics — only about the people they have gathered to see.
